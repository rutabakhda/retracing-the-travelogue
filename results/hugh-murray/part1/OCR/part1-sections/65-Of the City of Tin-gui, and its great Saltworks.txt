Tin-gui is a pretty agreeable city, a full day’s journey from Chaym. The people are idolaters, subject to the khan, and use paper money ; they have merchandise and arts, and numerous ships belonging to them. It lies to the south-east, and on the left, nearly three days’ journey to the eastward, is the ocean, where salt is made in great quantities, Here is a city namedCyn-gui, large, rich, and noble, to which all the salt is brought, and the khan draws from it a revenue so wonderful that it could not be believed.
